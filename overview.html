Introduction

Algeria is one of the largest African countries by area, with a rich history and an important political position regionally and internationally. This research provides an overview of Algeria, including its geography and history, then focuses on the political system and institutions, highlighting the role of citizens and youth in political life.

General information

Official name : People's Democratic Republic of Algeria
Capital: Algiers
Area :between 2.38 million km²
Population: Over 45 million inhabitants
Currency: Algerian Dinar
Official language: Arabic (Tamazight is also a national and official language)
Religion: Islam


Geographical location

Algeria is located in North Africa. It is bordered by the Mediterranean Sea to the north, Tunisia and Libya to the east, Morocco and Western Sahara to the west, and Mali and Niger to the south. The country has diverse climates and landscapes, including coastlines, mountains, and deserts.

Brief historical overview

Algeria experienced ancient civilizations and was colonized by France in 1830. The Algerian War of Independence began on November 1, 1954, and independence was achieved on July 5, 1962.

Political system

Algeria is a republic with a republican political system based on a constitution that organizes state powers and guarantees citizens' rights, following the principle of separation of powers.

State powers

Executive power

It is represented by the President of the Republic and the government, responsible for enforcing laws and managing state affairs

Legislative power

It is represented by Parliament with two chambers: the National People's Assembly and the Council of the Nation, responsible for legislation and oversight.

Judicial power

It ensures the application of laws and justice independently.

Political parties

Political parties play an important role in representing citizens, activating political life, and participating in elections.

Elections

Elections include presidential, legislative, and local elections, and are a fundamental means for citizens to participate in governance

Foreign policy

Algeria’s foreign policy is based on non-interference in internal affairs and supporting just causes.

Political challenges

Key challenges include enhancing political participation, providing opportunities for youth, and continuing reforms.

Role of youth

Youth are essential in building the future through awareness and political participation.

Conclusion

Algeria presents a political model based on institutions and the constitution, with citizens and youth playing a central role in political development.
